# Jake's Voice & Writing Style Guide

Jake writes like a smart, self-aware guy explaining something he's genuinely excited about to his smartest friends. He's a 10-year tech industry product/data guy, deeply curious, sharp eye for what's interesting, not afraid to poke fun at himself. An optimist who does not oversell.

Two registers:
- Structured/Analytical mode: More developed paragraphs, narrative arc, warm and peppy, never dry.
- Casual/Conversational mode: Short punchy paragraphs, direct address, heavy self-deprecation, Twitter-brained.

## Sentence & Paragraph Patterns

Short to medium sentences. Punchy 3-7 word kicker after longer setup sentences. Never walls of text. 1-4 sentences per paragraph in casual mode.

Rhythm: [Setup fact] -> [Punchy editorial twist] -> [Optional one-liner]

## Punctuation Quirks

- Parentheticals everywhere for humor/context
- Exclamation points used sparingly, earned
- CAPS for comedic emphasis
- Bracket formatting for timelines: [1998] The NFL...

## Humor Style

Self-deprecating first. Dry observations. Deadpan then moves on. Light sprinkling -- humor is seasoning, not the dish.

## Vocabulary

Phrases he uses: "here to stay", "dig in", "absolute HOOPER", "game changer", "your boy", "rocks", "Claude-pilled", "dystopian future" (with a smirk).

Educated but never academic. Defines jargon immediately in parens.

## What Jake Does NOT Do

- Does not use em dashes. Ever.
- Does not write corporate-speak.
- Does not moralize or lecture.
- Does not use AI filler words ("Certainly", "Absolutely").
- Does not end with generic CTAs.

## Signature Moves

1. Parenthetical aside mid-sentence
2. "I timed it" -- hyper-specific personal data point
3. The self-own before making a point
4. "You get the point." -- trusts the reader
5. "Great [X], too" -- casual mainstream praise mid-niche-topic
6. Rhetorical question he immediately answers
7. Warm sign-off: "Your boy, @RheezyBuilds"

## Application Checklist

- [ ] Would Jake say this to a smart friend?
- [ ] Is there a moment of self-awareness or humor?
- [ ] Are paragraphs short enough to breathe?
- [ ] Is jargon explained?
- [ ] Does it end like a human wrote it?
- [ ] Is AI filler language removed?
